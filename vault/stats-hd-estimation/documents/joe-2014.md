## Core Methodology
Comprehensive treatment of parametric copula families including: vine copulas (C-vines, D-vines, R-vines) for high-dimensional dependence modeling via pair-copula constructions; factor copulas; and copula-based models for discrete and mixed data. Covers maximum likelihood estimation, inference functions for margins (IFM), and model selection via information criteria.

## Key Results
- Vine copulas decompose a d-dimensional copula into d(d-1)/2 bivariate copulas arranged in a tree structure
- This decomposition allows different dependence structures for different pairs — far more flexible than elliptical or exchangeable Archimedean copulas
- Factor copulas provide a parsimonious alternative for high-dimensional settings with latent structure
- Student-t copula's tail dependence coefficient = 2·t_{ν+1}(-√((ν+1)(1-ρ)/(1+ρ))), decreasing toward 0 as ν → ∞
- IFM (inference functions for margins) estimation is consistent but less efficient than full MLE
- AIC/BIC model selection between copula families is valid but can be unreliable with small samples

## Stated Limitations
- Vine copula estimation requires O(d²) bivariate parameters — high-dimensional estimation is data-hungry
- Vine structure selection (which tree?) is combinatorially complex — heuristics used in practice may miss the optimal structure
- Tail dependence estimation from the Student-t copula requires reliable estimation of degrees of freedom — unreliable with n < 500 for moderate dimensions
- Model selection between copula families using AIC/BIC can be inconclusive when data does not clearly favor one family
- Parametric copula models are inherently misspecified — no finite-parameter copula captures the true dependence of real data

## Applicability Conditions
- Student-t copula appropriate when: symmetric tail dependence is present and sample is large enough to estimate ν reliably
- Vine copulas appropriate when: dimension > 3 and different pairs have different dependence structures
- Factor copulas appropriate when: latent structure drives dependence and dimension is large
- Key caution: degrees of freedom estimation in t-copula is unreliable with small samples — misestimation can produce near-Gaussian or unrealistically heavy-tailed behavior
